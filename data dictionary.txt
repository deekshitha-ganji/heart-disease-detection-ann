1.  Age: Age of the patient in years.
2.  Sex: Gender of the patient. 1 represents male, 0 represents female.
3.  CP (Chest Pain Type): Type of chest pain experienced by the patient.
	0: Typical angina
	1: Atypical angina
	2: Non-anginal pain
	3: Asymptomatic
4.  Trestbps: Resting blood pressure of the patient in mm Hg on admission to the hospital.
5.  Chol: Serum cholesterol level in mg/dl.
6.  FBS (Fasting Blood Sugar): Whether the patient's fasting blood sugar is above 120 mg/dl. 1 represents true, 0 represents false.
7.  Restecg (Resting Electrocardiographic Results): Results of the resting electrocardiogram.
	0: Normal
	1: ST-T wave abnormality (T wave inversions and/or ST elevation or depression of > 0.05 mV)
	2: Probable or definite left ventricular hypertrophy by Estes' criteria
8.  Thalach: Maximum heart rate achieved.
9.  Exang (Exercise Induced Angina): Whether the patient experienced exercise-induced angina. 1 represents yes, 0 represents no.
10. Oldpeak: ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest.
11. Slope: The slope of the peak exercise ST segment.
	0: Upsloping
	1: Flat
	2: Downsloping
12. Ca (Number of Major Vessels): Number of major vessels (0-3) colored by fluoroscopy.
13. Thal: Thalassemia type.
	0: Error
	1: Fixed defect
	2: Normal
	3: Reversible defect
14. Target: The label indicating whether the patient has heart disease.
	0: No disease
	1: Disease
